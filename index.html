Single-Message Plot: General Guidance

  • Central value of a single-message categorical chart is clarity of interpretation
  • Visual makes the intended insight immediately clear
  • Categorical plots guide the viewer toward a decision or insight

Study Background

What is CAR-T cell therapy?

  • CAR-T cell therapy: form of immunotherapy that leverages a patient’s immune system to fight cancer
  • Researchers genetically engineer a patient’s T cells to express receptors (chimeric antigen receptors - CARs) that recognize specific antigens on cancer cells
  • Genetically re-engineered T-cells (CAR-T cells) are re-infused into the patient where the T cells seek out and destroy tumor cells
  • Often used to treat: hematologic cancers like acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) and diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL)

Chohan KL, et al. (2023). Curr Hematol Malig Rep.

Study Background

How are adverse events measured?

  • CAR-T therapy is a form of immunotherapy that stimulates the immune system to attack cancer that can have a range of side effects:
  • Grades 1–5 refer to the severity of adverse events or toxicities:
  • Grade 1 (Mild): minimal symptoms; does not require intervention (i.e.: mild rash)
  • Grade 2 (Moderate): symptoms are more noticeable and may require minimal medical intervention (moderate nausea)

Study Background

How are adverse events measured?

  • Grade 3 (Severe): medically significant and requires intervention, possibly hospitalization (i.e.: severe organ dysfunction)
  • Grade 4 (Life-Threatening): urgent, life-threatening consequences; requires immediate medical intervention (i.e.: respiratory failure)
  • Grade 5 (Death): toxicity results in patient death

Research Question

  • What is the distribution and severity of toxicities experienced by patients receiving CAR-T cell therapy?
  • What proportion experience low-grade versus high-grade events?

Categories flow in a way that reinforces the central point

Visual Guidance

Summary

When developing a single-message figure:

Visual that provides clarity of interpretation;

  • Viewer instantly understands the main insight

Make the Intended Insight Immediately Clear:

  • Before plotting, ask: “What is the single insight I want my audience to take away?”

  • The entire visual structure — the arrangement of categories, ordering, and supporting elements — should make this insight obvious at first glance
